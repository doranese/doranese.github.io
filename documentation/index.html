Doranese Documentation

Doranese is a language intended to use as few latin base characters in a different writing syllabary. The following characters are the eleven characters of the language. Accented sounds, such as nasal sounds, do not apply to the final count, as they are simply modifications to an existing sound

  • A a
    • A˩ a˩ -- Long low tone
    • A˧ a˧ -- Long medium tone
    • A˦ a˦ -- Long high tone
    • Ã˩ ã˩ -- Long nasal low tone
    • Ã˧ ã˧ -- Long nasal medium tone
    • Ã˦ ã˦ -- Long nasal high tone
  • E e
    • E˩ e˩ -- Long low tone
    • E˧ e˧ -- Long medium tone
    • E˦ e˦ -- Long high tone
    • Ẽ˩ ẽ˩ -- Long nasal low tone
    • Ẽ˧ ẽ˧ -- Long nasal medium tone
    • Ẽ˦ ẽ˦ -- Long nasal high tone
  • I i
    • I˩ i˩ -- Long low tone
    • I˧ i˧ -- Long medium tone
    • I˦ i˦ -- Long high tone
    • Ĩ˩ ĩ˩ -- Long nasal low tone
    • Ĩ˧ ĩ˧ -- Long nasal medium tone
    • Ĩ˦ ĩ˦ -- Long nasal high tone
  • O o
    • O˩ o˩ -- Long low tone
    • O˧ o˧ -- Long medium tone
    • O˦ o˦ -- Long high tone
    • Õ˩ õ˩ -- Long nasal low tone
    • Õ˧ õ˧ -- Long nasal medium tone
    • Õ˦ õ˦ -- Long nasal high tone
  • U u
    • U˩ u˩ -- Long low tone
    • U˧ u˧ -- Long medium tone
    • U˦ u˦ -- Long high tone
    • Ũ˩ ũ˩ -- Long nasal low tone
    • Ũ˧ ũ˧ -- Long nasal medium tone
    • Ũ˦ ũ˦ -- Long nasal high tone
  • B b
    • ƀ -- Produces an aerial "B" sound
  • D d
    • đ -- Produces an aerial "D" sound
  • J j
    • J̌ ǰ -- Produces a "Zh" sound like the "s" in the word pleasure
  • S s
    • Š š -- Produces a "Sh" sound
  • N n
    • Ñ ñ -- Produces a "Ni" sound, such as in the English word "Nit"
  • R r
    • <R> <r> -- Produces a trilled "R" sound
    • R̃ r̃ -- Produces a nasal "R" sound, such as in the end of the French word "Bonjour"
